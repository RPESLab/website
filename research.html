Research

Many exciting research projects on power systems, renewable energy, electricity market design, and the integration of advanced technologies

Theories and Approaches of Complex Power and Energy Systems

Advanced research in complex power and energy systems integrates multidisciplinary approaches from power systems, power electronics, thermal power, optimization and control, information and communication, economics and management science, and artificial intelligence. Original innovations have been achieved in the modeling, analysis, optimization, and control of these systems, with special emphasis on engineering applications. Over 10 national key projects have been executed, more than 300 papers have been published, and over 20 national invention patents have been secured in China.

Innovative Algorithms and Methodologies

A breakthrough in this field is the development of the cooperative co-evolutionary algorithm for power system unit commitment, which has evolved into an essential tool for power system operation, planning, control, and electricity market simulation. Pioneering research in electricity pricing theory and market design—utilizing mathematical optimization, game theory, swarm intelligence, and experimental economics—has provided new insights into electricity market structures. These innovations are especially significant in the context of the energy transition, offering fresh perspectives for market reform and addressing challenges such as the current European energy crisis.

Integrated Energy Networks and IoT Applications

The establishment of a fundamental theory of energy networks that unifies different energy forms lays a solid foundation for planning and operating integrated energy systems. Complementary contributions to the information processing of the power/energy Internet of Things (IoT) have further advanced digital solutions for modern power systems. These interdisciplinary theories and technologies are exemplified in the design and development of (Guangzhou) Virtual Power Plants.

Recognition and Ongoing Challenges

The theoretical study of complex systems has merged into mainstream science—as highlighted by the Nobel Prize in Physics 2021, awarded for groundbreaking contributions to understanding complex systems. The cooperative co-evolutionary algorithm, among other methods, has been rigorously tested over time, demonstrating its effectiveness and advantages. The challenge of “how to cognize and construct new power systems from the perspective of complex systems” has been identified as a major issue for the power sector, guiding ongoing research efforts.

Electricity Market Design and Simulation

Research that combines power systems, microeconomics, and mathematical optimization has led to innovative electricity pricing theories, including models based on the continuous-time commodity approach. Such work is crucial for the design of electricity markets amid the rapid development of renewable energy, increased demands for flexibility, and global energy challenges. The resulting market design and pricing schemes have been widely implemented in electricity market simulation systems across more than 10 provinces in China, influencing public opinion and government policy.


New Energy Power Systems


Research in new energy power systems focuses on the planning, operation, control, and pricing of renewable energy power systems. The research integrates multiple fields, including power systems, power electronics, and automation, to develop innovative technologies and products. A key area of investigation involves robust scheduling and dispatch models based on the concept of "extreme scenario," with extensions to robust transmission planning.

The concept of cooperative control has been developed to solve dynamic coordination issues in power systems, particularly in multi-controller environments such as frequency controllers. This theory has been successfully applied in the world’s first AC power grid breakup engineering in the China Southern Power Grid.

A notable advancement is the development of the first multiple-time-frame robust scheduling/dispatch system, which has been implemented at the national Smart Grid demonstration base on Hainan Island. These scheduling and dispatch methods have been applied to China Southern Power Grid and other power grids to better integrate renewable energy.

In addition, new methods have been introduced for solving the interval power flow problem, where demand load and renewable energy generation are treated as interval uncertainties. This has led to the development of interval Volt/Var control for power systems under uncertainty, which is especially useful when dealing with significant intermittent power penetration. A new electricity pricing scheme and market design have also been proposed to accommodate power systems with high shares of variable renewable energy.

Over the past decade, extensive research has been conducted on renewable energy generation control and other digital solutions for the power and energy industry. Technologies and products resulting from this research have been implemented across more than 20 provinces in China and exported to Southeast Asian countries.

The research has garnered attention for its innovative models and methods, such as robust scheduling and cooperative control. These methods have been widely cited and further developed by other researchers. The work was featured on CCTV-10 as part of the "New Energy Power" program (August 7, 2019) and August 8, 2019.

Contributions to teaching in electricity markets and renewable energy have reached over 1 million attendees through online and TV lectures, further spreading knowledge in these fields.

Recent research has focused on national energy transition projects, particularly in the Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macao Greater Bay Area. Several key national and provincial projects have been undertaken, including the National Natural Science Foundation, the National Funds for Excellent Young Scientists, and other initiatives supported by China Southern Power Grid Co. (CSG).

Technologies developed in these projects include main control systems for wind turbines, wind/solar power prediction systems, multiple-time-frame robust scheduling/dispatch systems, intelligent monitoring and maintenance systems for wind farms, microgrid controllers, and energy management systems. These innovations have been applied in over 20 provinces across China and exported to Southeast Asian countries.
